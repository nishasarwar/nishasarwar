Carbon Accounting

Developing frameworks and data-driven methods to accurately measure, track, and report carbon emissions — enabling organizations to make informed decisions toward net-zero goals.

Status
Ongoing
Timeline
2023 - Present
Role
Data Scientist & Engineer

Overview

Organizations today face increasing pressure to measure and reduce their carbon footprint. However, accurate carbon accounting is complex — spanning Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions, integrating multiple data sources, and requiring sophisticated modeling. This project develops practical frameworks and tools to help organizations understand their emissions landscape and track progress toward sustainability goals.

Problem Statement

Most organizations lack accurate, complete carbon accounting. Data is siloed across departments, methodologies are inconsistent, and baseline emissions are difficult to establish. Without proper measurement, organizations cannot set credible reduction targets or verify progress. This project addresses these gaps through:

  • Data integration from disparate sources (energy bills, travel records, supply chain data)
  • Standardized emission factor databases and methodologies
  • Automated tracking and reporting workflows
  • Scenario modeling for carbon reduction strategies
  • Third-party verification and audit trails

Methodological Approach

We follow established carbon accounting standards including:

  • GHG Protocol Corporate Accounting and Reporting Standard
  • ISO 14064-1 Greenhouse Gas Quantification and Reporting
  • Scope-based classification (Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions)
  • Primary data collection and secondary emission factors
  • Uncertainty quantification and data quality assessment

Key Features

  • Data Pipeline: Automated ingestion and validation of emissions data
  • Emission Factors: Curated databases of carbon intensities by region and sector
  • Calculation Engine: Flexible, auditable emissions computation
  • Reporting: Executive dashboards and detailed technical reports
  • Forecasting: Baseline, BAU, and scenario projections

Use Cases

  • Tech companies tracking Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions from operations and supply chain
  • Manufacturers optimizing production processes for lower carbon intensity
  • Financial institutions assessing climate risk in portfolios
  • Government agencies monitoring sectoral progress toward climate targets
