High Performance Computing

Investigating the efficiency and scalability of HPC systems, with a focus on workload optimization and understanding the environmental footprint of large-scale computation.

Status
Active Research
Timeline
2024 - Present
Role
Graduate Researcher

Overview

High-performance computing systems power everything from climate simulations to machine learning training to scientific discovery. Yet HPC systems consume enormous amounts of energy. This research investigates how to optimize HPC workloads for efficiency without sacrificing performance, while understanding the broader environmental implications of large-scale computation.

Research Focus

The project addresses three key dimensions:

  • Efficiency: How can we measure and optimize energy efficiency across HPC systems?
  • Scalability: What are the scaling laws for energy consumption as we add more compute?
  • Environmental Impact: What is the true carbon cost of HPC, including hardware manufacturing, deployment, and disposal?

Technical Investigation

  • Performance monitoring and profiling of HPC workloads
  • Power measurement and energy-aware scheduling
  • Hardware selection and procurement optimization
  • Cooling system design and efficiency
  • Data center operations and load balancing
  • Lifecycle assessment of HPC infrastructure

Key Findings

Our research demonstrates that significant efficiency gains are possible through intelligent workload scheduling, frequency scaling, and hardware selection. However, many organizations operate HPC systems without comprehensive energy monitoring — leaving considerable optimization potential on the table.

Practical Applications

  • Guidelines for energy-efficient HPC cluster design
  • Tools for workload profiling and optimization
  • Benchmarking frameworks for comparative system evaluation
  • Carbon accounting models for research institutions
